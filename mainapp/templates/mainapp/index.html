    Problems of Today

    • According to a report by Down to Earth, 5.7% of the senior citizens in India lives alone
    • One of the major reason of elderly living alone is lack of working opportunities in the hometowns, especially rural
    • According to India Skills Report 2022 by Wheebox, only 48.7% of total youth in India is employable
    • 75% of companies surveyed reported a skill gap in the industry
    • 74% Indians suffering from stress, 88% from anxiety
    • 68.6% therapists reported an increase in the number of people they see on a daily basis since covid-19 pandemic outbreak
    • Be it municipal waste, plastics or complex waste such as electronic, batteries, etc., despite all the efforts in policy-making it still seems to be one of the most challenging times of the Indian history
    • According to a report by Niti Aayog, less than 20% of the waste is treated/recycled in India

    Problems of Tomorrow

    • Lifestyle diseases are breaking all the age barriers, children way below the age group of 20 are suffering from stress, immune disorders, heartache, blood pressure, diabetes, etc.
    • Life expectancy in India is far below the global average, it stands at 69.66 years
    • More complex waste streams are emerging as the older technologies are becoming obsolete, for instance, India started gearing up for solar and li-ion battery recycling, though, technologies to 100% recycle them are not yet available
    • It is estimated that complex waste will grow 30-fold in next 10 years
    • It is estimated that 85% of the jobs that will exist in 2030 haven’t been invented yet
    • Employee retention rate is too low and is falling. Companies are unable to spot talent and even if they find, employees don’t stay
    • We are well aware of the depleting resources, not just that, there’s a urban housing shortage of 54% which is ever-increasing
    • Flash floods happening in cities like Gurugram, Bangalore, Chennai, etc. More than that it’s about traffic, pollution, stress on the urban infrastructure, etc.

    Problems that never fade out

    • By 2030, 50% of India’s population is expected to live in the urban cities accelerating problems of overcrowding, housing, unemployment, squatter settlement, transport, sewage, trash, drinking water, pollution, etc.
    • Migrant workers in urban India faces several issues in searching employment, companies exploit them in wages, insurances etc. and most often they end up in debts due to high cost of living, etc.
    • Indian Government is working well in order to design policies (such as extended producers responsibility, corporate social responsibility, logistics, UPI, etc. but implementation of it in a country like India is a tedious task due to various known factors
    • Lack of resources and lobbying by the powerful corporate houses is another big challenge in good governance
