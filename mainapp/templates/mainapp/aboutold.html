About Us


People in the Himalayas believe that there’s a snowman that is covered with brown, grey or white hair and is sometimes depicted as having large, sharp teeth. They call it Yeti, living in Himalayas, thus Himalayan Yeti!
We don’t know, we have never seen a Yeti, but we have seen plastics in the Himalayas, we have seen irresponsible tourists coming in, boozing, partying and imbalancing the natural habitat.
What we have also seen is the youth migrating to urban cities leaving behind their land, fertile soil, their problems and hardships, and also their parents.
Leaving behind everything can never be questionable, isn’t it? Certainly it is, as what they’ve left is diamond in the search of gold, and today half of them, half of us, you, me all of us, arent happy or satisfied living in the concrete jungles
There are ample opportunities available in the rural India which is unexplored and if approached in a righteous way, it can help us reunite rural families and open an array of possibilities and scope for development
Himalayan Yeti, a section 8 company registered under the companies act of 2013, having 12A and 80G status is a foundation for meaningful purpose. The organization was established in 2019 by Nagender Singh for the sole purpose to fight plastic menace in Ladakh region, but with time, our mission changed and became more holistic.
Today our mission is to Bring the Youth Back and help them rehabilitate in their rural villages with their families, and create opportunities for them, so that they can be innovators, entrepreneurs, skilled-professionals, etc.
